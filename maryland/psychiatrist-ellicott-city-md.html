About this practice

Care that understands what excellence costs.

Ellicott City is home to some of the best public schools in the nation and to a large, close-knit Korean American community, along with many Chinese, Indian, and Vietnamese families. Communities built around education and achievement produce remarkable outcomes, and a very specific kind of private suffering: perfectionism, socially prescribed pressure, and the belief that your worth is your report card, long after school ends.

Alice Tran grew up inside that same equation as the eldest daughter of Vietnamese immigrants. She treats the anxiety, depression, and burnout that high-expectation families produce, in adults who often look outwardly flawless. Asian American patients consistently underuse mental health care; this practice was built to lower every barrier it can.

Ellicott City has also weathered real collective hardship, including devastating floods on Main Street, and resilience is part of this town’s identity. Resilience and needing support are not opposites. Appointments are by secure telehealth anywhere in Maryland, in English or Tiếng Việt, with most insurance accepted.

Quick answers

What Ellicott City patients want to know

Do you understand Korean family culture even though you are Vietnamese?

The specifics differ and Alice never pretends otherwise, but the core pressures, filial piety, academic expectation, face, and stigma, run closely parallel. Patients from Korean families say the shared framework matters more than the shared language.

Can my visits stay completely private from my family?

Yes. You are the patient, records are confidential, and telehealth means no one sees you walk into an office. What you share with family is entirely your choice.

Is medication the only thing you offer?

No. Evaluations, supportive therapy, and medication management are all part of the practice, and plenty of patients use the evaluation to understand themselves before deciding anything.

How quickly can I get an appointment?

Usually within one to two weeks, by secure video from home, campus, or work.
