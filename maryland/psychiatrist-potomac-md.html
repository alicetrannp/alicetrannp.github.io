About this practice

When you are the one everyone else relies on.

In a community like Potomac, the barrier to mental health care is rarely cost or access. It is exposure. Physicians worry about licensing boards, executives about boards of directors, and everyone about the country club grapevine. So high-achieving adults self-manage: more exercise, more wine, more work, while the underlying anxiety or depression compounds quietly for years.

Research on high achievers is blunt: they experience depression and anxiety at rates equal to or above the general population and are among the least likely to seek help. Perfectionism, the very trait that built the career, is one of the strongest predictors of hidden suffering. None of this improves on its own, and all of it responds to competent, confidential treatment.

This practice offers exactly that: unhurried evaluations by a dual board-certified clinician, treatment reasoning explained at the level you operate at, and secure telehealth so care fits between a morning case and an afternoon board call. No waiting rooms, no chance encounters, no drama.

Executives & physicians

People whose competence is their currency, and who need a clinician comfortable saying hard things clearly and briefly.

High-functioning depression

The grayness behind a successful life. Persistent depressive disorder can run for decades unnoticed, and it treats well.

Anxiety that masquerades as drive

Relentless standards, 4 a.m. wake-ups, the inability to ever be off. There is a difference between ambition and untreated anxiety.

Adult ADHD at the top

Plenty of brilliant careers are built on white-knuckled compensation. An accurate diagnosis often explains a lifetime.

Quick answers

What Potomac patients want to know

How discreet is this, really?

Telehealth from your home or office, HIPAA-protected records, and a solo clinician rather than a large group practice. If you have specific concerns about documentation, raise them at the first visit and Alice will answer plainly.

I am a physician. Will a diagnosis affect my license?

Alice will not pretend the concern is irrational, and she is careful and transparent about records. Untreated depression is far more dangerous to a career than treated depression; how to navigate disclosure questions is a conversation she is glad to have.

I do not have ninety minutes for appointments.

The evaluation is thorough because accuracy requires it, but follow-ups are efficient, typically thirty minutes, scheduled to your calendar, joined from wherever you are.

Do you take insurance, or is this concierge?

Most major insurance plans are accepted, including Aetna, Anthem, and Cigna, with transparent self-pay rates for those who prefer not to use insurance. See Rates.
