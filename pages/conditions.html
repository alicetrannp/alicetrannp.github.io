Who I help

You don't have to name it
perfectly to be welcome here.

These are some of the experiences people bring to a first visit. You're welcome if you see yourself in one, many, or none of them.

I work with adults ages 18–60 across Virginia through telehealth. Whether your concerns are longstanding or new, I offer a full first hour to understand your history, symptoms, and what matters most to you before we decide together on next steps.

🌀

Anxiety & Panic

Generalized anxiety disorder, panic disorder, social anxiety, and health anxiety can all make daily life feel exhausting and unpredictable. Together we'll work to understand your anxiety patterns and build a plan that gives you back a sense of calm and control.

  • Generalized Anxiety Disorder (GAD)
  • Panic Disorder
  • Social Anxiety Disorder
  • Health Anxiety
  • Separation Anxiety
🌧

Depression

Depression looks different for everyone — it can be persistent sadness, numbness, loss of motivation, or simply feeling unlike yourself. You deserve care that addresses the full picture, not just the surface.

  • Major Depressive Disorder
  • Persistent Depressive Disorder (Dysthymia)
  • Seasonal Affective Disorder
  • Situational Depression
⚡

ADHD

ADHD in adults is often missed or misunderstood. Whether you've known since childhood or are just now wondering if it explains years of struggle, a thoughtful evaluation and a personalized treatment approach can make a real difference.

  • ADHD — Inattentive Type
  • ADHD — Hyperactive-Impulsive Type
  • ADHD — Combined Presentation
🕊

Trauma & PTSD

Traumatic experiences — whether from the past or ongoing — can show up in the body, in relationships, and in your daily sense of safety. Care here is paced, collaborative, and always at your comfort level.

  • Post-Traumatic Stress Disorder (PTSD)
  • Acute Stress Disorder
  • Complex Trauma (C-PTSD)
  • Childhood Adversity & ACEs
🔁

OCD

Obsessive-Compulsive Disorder is more than just habits or preferences — it can feel relentless and intrusive. Evidence-based approaches can bring meaningful relief.

  • Obsessive-Compulsive Disorder
  • Intrusive Thoughts
  • Compulsive Behaviors
🌓

Bipolar & Mood Disorders

Living with mood instability can be isolating, especially when the highs and lows affect work, relationships, and sense of self. Careful diagnosis and consistent care make a difference.

  • Bipolar I & II Disorder
  • Cyclothymia
  • Mood Dysregulation
🤱

Postpartum & Perinatal Mood

The transition to parenthood can be one of the most emotionally complex experiences of your life. Postpartum depression and anxiety are common, treatable, and nothing to be ashamed of.

  • Postpartum Depression
  • Postpartum Anxiety
  • Perinatal Mood Disorders
  • Prenatal Depression & Anxiety
😴

Insomnia & Sleep

Poor sleep is both a symptom and a driver of mental health struggles. Addressing sleep directly — through therapy and when appropriate, medication — is part of whole-person care.

  • Chronic Insomnia
  • Sleep Anxiety
  • Sleep Disturbance related to Mood or Trauma
🔥

Burnout & Life Transitions

Sometimes the ground shifts — a new job, a move, grief, a relationship ending, or just the accumulation of too much for too long. Burnout and transition stress are real, valid, and worth caring for.

  • Burnout
  • Caregiver Fatigue
  • Grief & Loss
  • Major Life Transitions
  • Adjustment Disorder
🌏

Cultural & Identity-Related Stress

The pressures of navigating two cultures, family expectations, immigration, and identity can quietly take a toll. This is a space where all of that is welcome and understood.

  • Intergenerational & Cultural Conflict
  • Acculturation Stress
  • Identity & Belonging
  • International Student Stress
  • Immigration-Related Anxiety
💢

Anger & Emotional Dysregulation

Difficulty managing emotions doesn't mean something is permanently wrong with you — it often signals that something important hasn't been addressed yet. We'll work on it together.

  • Anger Management
  • Emotional Dysregulation
  • Impulse Control Difficulties
  • Self-Esteem & Boundary Work
A gentle note

Not sure if your concern is on this list?

This list isn't exhaustive. If something is weighing on you and you're wondering whether psychiatric care might help, reach out. The first conversation is simply that — a conversation.
