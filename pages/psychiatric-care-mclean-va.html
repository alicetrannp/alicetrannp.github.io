About this practice

McLean has every resource imaginable. Mental health support should be one of them.

McLean sits at the edge of the Beltway and the Potomac, home to executives, government officials, attorneys, diplomats, and some of the most high-functioning adults in the region. The schools are competitive. The careers are demanding. The homes are well-kept. And beneath all of that, the internal experience is not always as composed as the exterior.

Anxiety that never quite shuts off. Depression that coexists with a full schedule. ADHD that has been managed through sheer willpower for decades. Burnout that looks like productivity from the outside. These are things Alice sees regularly, and they are no less real for happening in a wealthy zip code.

Alice Tran, PMHNP-BC provides telehealth psychiatric care for adults in McLean and the surrounding area, including Great Falls, Langley, Tysons, and the Route 123 corridor. She is board-certified in psychiatric mental health, sees patients in English and Vietnamese, and provides full evaluations, medication management, and supportive therapy via secure video.

Anxiety & burnout

High-functioning anxiety, executive burnout, and the particular exhaustion of carrying enormous responsibility for a long time without support.

Depression

Including the kind that coexists with a full life. Medication, therapy, or both, depending on what you need and what you have already tried.

ADHD

Many high-achieving adults in demanding careers reach an ADHD diagnosis in their 30s, 40s, or later. Comprehensive evaluation and treatment available.

Life transitions

Career changes, retirement, divorce, loss. When the structure of a well-organized life shifts, the ground underneath can feel less solid than expected.

Quick answers

What McLean patients want to know

What insurance do you accept?

See Rates for most updated in network insurances and current fees.

Do you treat children or teenagers?

Alice's practice is for adults ages 18 to 65. For younger patients, she is happy to provide a referral recommendation if you reach out directly.

Is everything done by video? I travel frequently.

Yes, all appointments are fully telehealth via secure video. As long as you are physically in Virginia during your appointment, you can join from wherever you are, home, hotel, or office.

Do I need a referral or a prior diagnosis?

No referral is needed and no prior diagnosis is required. The first appointment is the evaluation. You do not need to arrive knowing what is wrong.
